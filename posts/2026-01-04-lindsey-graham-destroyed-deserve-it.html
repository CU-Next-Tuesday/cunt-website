What was said

In May 2016, Sen. Lindsey Graham posted a warning about the GOP nominating Donald Trump: the party would “get destroyed”… and “deserve it.”

What happened next

After Trump won the nomination and then the presidency, Graham’s posture shifted from critic to ally. The “spine check” here isn’t about whether anyone likes him — it’s about the documented reversal from public warning to public protection.

Receipts timeline

Bottom line

If you publicly say the nominee will wreck the party — and then you become the nominee’s shield — that’s the definition of a Spine Check entry.
