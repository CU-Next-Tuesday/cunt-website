Environment: EPA’s Clean Water Act §401 memo and what it signals

2026-01-10 receipts environment rulemaking clean water act section 401 timeline receipts

What happened: EPA issued a memorandum on the scope of §401 certification (May 21, 2025).
Why it matters: §401 is a leverage point for states/Tribes in federally-permitted projects; narrowing its scope shifts power in permitting disputes.

Timeline

2023
EPA’s §401 certification rule issued (baseline referenced by EPA).
2025-05-21
EPA issues memo clarifying the limited role states/Tribes play in §401 certification.
2025-05-22
E&E News reports EPA warning against using §401 to block energy projects (memo context).

EPA’s own Section 401 page documents a May 21, 2025 memorandum on the scope of certification, describing it as clarifying the significant but limited role states and Tribes play under §401 in federal licensing/permitting.

EPA also issued a related press release framing the action as aligning §401 with its broader agenda; the framing matters because it signals intent and priorities, even when the legal authority is grounded in the Clean Water Act.

E&E News (Politico) reported on the memo as a warning that states and tribes cannot use §401 as a ‘weapon’ to block energy projects. The receipts question is simple: what changed operationally, and what do states/Tribes do next (litigation, guidance, permitting behavior)?
